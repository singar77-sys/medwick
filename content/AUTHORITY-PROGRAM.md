# Medwick Construction: The Authority Program (the 80%)

The website is roughly 20% of the job. This is the other 80%: the authority that lives outside the website, where the site is simply where it accumulates and gets measured. A competitor can copy a homepage in a weekend. They cannot copy this, because it is not marketing, it is what the company does.

> Per `POSITIONING.md`, Medwick wins by becoming the source homeowners, adjusters, reporters, Google, and AI systems cite. That trust is earned off-site and displayed on-site. The Authority Program is how it is earned; the website (see `SITE-STRUCTURE.md`) is where it lands.

## Why this is the bigger half

Ranking for "roofing Medina" is expensive, slow, and crowded. Being the trusted answer when a homeowner's ceiling is leaking is cheaper, faster, and defensible. The reports show every competitor is weak on exactly the signals this program builds: reviews, local identity, educational content, and AI visibility. None of them are earned by the website alone.

## The nine workstreams

1. **Review velocity.** Every completed job, every platform, an FTC-compliant ask (no incentives, no gating). Named rungs: clear Fixler's 34 (the roofing identity leader), then pass 50 in the first six months, on the way toward SERVPRO's 189. Review recency and pace are a top local and AI ranking signal, and the field is barely competing on it.
2. **Real project documentation.** Nothing leaves a job without before photos, after photos, drone photos, a short video, and a homeowner testimonial. This is the Documentation pillar from the positioning. One shoot becomes a site case study, a GBP post, social content, and the proof inside answer pages.
3. **Storm-reactive publishing.** Within 48 hours of any NWS-warned severe event, the pre-built playbook fires: a "hit by the {date} storm in {towns}, here is what to do" post plus a mirrored GBP post. This is the single fastest way to seed the hail-damage and emergency queries, and no incumbent does it.
4. **Google Business Profile.** Claimed and verified, categories and attributes tuned for AI retrieval, weekly posts, fresh photos, and a maintained Q&A. The GBP is the top driver of local calls and a primary source AI answers pull from.
5. **Insurance relationships.** The flagship advocacy position, made real: adjuster and agent relationships, a published "we work for homeowners, not insurance companies" process, and a referral network. This is the moat competitors sell around but do not own.
6. **Local sponsorships and awards.** Greater Medina Chamber, the Home and Garden Show, the Medina County Fair, youth sports. Run an organized push at the Medina Gazette Reader's Choice and "Best of Medina": both are community-vote awards that reward the most engaged customer base, and the restoration categories are effectively unclaimed.
7. **Citations and NAP.** One byte-identical name, address, and phone across BBB, Yelp, Angi, HomeAdvisor, Nextdoor, Apple Maps, and Bing Places. Consistent NAP is the base signal answer engines read to resolve one trusted entity.
8. **Certifications, documented.** IICRC WRT/ASD then Certified Firm for water, GAF or Owens Corning tier for roofing. Every claim documented before it is stated, in deliberate contrast to the two roofing competitors carrying open BBB problems over unsubstantiated claims. Reconstruction rides on general-contracting scope, so no discretionary-remodeling credential arms race is required.
9. **Provable fast response.** Measure and publish a real in-county response window the North Royalton, Akron, and Cleveland regionals physically cannot match. In an emergency category, a proven arrival time turns a category cliche into a hard differentiator.

## The cadence: who does what, how often

- **Every job:** photo and video set, review ask, testimonial. Crew captures, owner follows up.
- **Every week:** one GBP post, three geo-tagged social posts, one answer-first guide (see `CONTENT-CALENDAR.md`).
- **Every storm:** the playbook fires in 24 to 48 hours.
- **Every quarter:** a sponsorship or award push, a citation and NAP audit, and progress on the next certification.

## How it compounds on the website

Every off-site asset has an on-site home: reviews feed the reviews page and review schema, photos feed project case studies and the GBP, answers feed the Learning Center and the AI-citation layer, and insurance relationships feed the claims hub. The website is where authority accumulates, and the analytics are where it is measured.

## The honest expectation

The website can be built in weeks. The authority is a 6 to 12 month compounding effort. Reviews and storm-reactive publishing are the fastest levers, so they start on day one, before the new site is even live (see Phase 0 in `IMPLEMENTATION-ROADMAP.md`). The website is necessary, but it is not the work. This is the work.
