# Medwick Construction: Content Calendar

Publishing plan for the rebuild, sequenced to Medwick's position as Medina County's Insurance Restoration Advocate. Roofing, water mitigation, and reconstruction are the services. Insurance advocacy is the brand, and this calendar exists to make the website an authority engine: every asset answers exactly one decision a homeowner faces after property damage, from a leaking ceiling to a flooded basement to a denied claim. The goal is authority and AI citation, not keyword chasing.

The build window (mid-July to mid-October 2026) sits inside peak wind season (August is busiest) and rides the September "roof repair near me" national peak. Winter leak and pipe content must index **before** the January leak-repair spike, so it ships by late October.

The single largest opening is the same across every service line: **nobody answers the questions homeowners actually type after damage, and nobody owns the insurance decision that follows.** Everyone bills insurance; no one has claimed the "you're our customer, not the insurance company's" position, and no competitor is the cited answer when a homeowner asks an AI who to call. That makes advocacy-led answer content the top priority, because one well-built asset earns AI-search citation and durable organic authority at once. Even 12 to 20 strong Medina-specific assets build a lead no incumbent can quickly close.

## Priority order

1. **Insurance-claim advocacy content:** the flagship, and the verified-empty niche across roofing and water. Own the homeowner's decision after damage: does insurance cover this, can I choose my own contractor, what do I do when a claim is denied, how do I read the adjuster's scope. This is the brand made into pages, and it feeds both AI citation and organic authority.
2. **Storm-damage answer content:** the second flagship. Hail, wind, tree strikes, emergency tarping, and "what do I do right now" content, each tied to the insurance path that follows. Storm and insurance lead together because they are where the advocate position is won.
3. **Water and emergency answer content:** the softest, most content-dark market, where no local advocate exists. Sump-pump failure, sewage backup, burst pipe first hour, how fast mold grows, who to call. Direct answers, one asset per query.
4. **Localized cost guides:** the format that proved it ranks. Medina-specific, answer-engine-tuned pricing for water cleanup, roof replacement, and reconstruction after a covered loss, always framed by what insurance does and does not pay.
5. **Storm-response posts:** within 48 hours of NWS-warned events (the proven accelerator).
6. **Project case studies + review asks:** every job becomes a site case study, a GBP post, and social, each closing with a review request. This is the review-velocity engine, not a side effect.
7. **Winter content:** roof leaks, ice dams, frozen pipes. Live by late October.
8. **Houzz cadence (optional, future):** discretionary remodel promotion on Houzz is not Medwick's play under the advocacy position. Hold it as an optional future track only if a deliberate remodeling push is ever greenlit.

## How content ties to reviews and awards

Content and review velocity compound: every case study and answer post ends with a review ask, because review volume is the single fastest authority lever. The targets are concrete: beat Fixler's 34 reviews inside the first stretch, then approach SERVPRO's 189, aiming for 50 Google reviews in six months. The award pushes ride the same content: sustained local roofing and storm content plus review velocity feed the **Medina Gazette Reader's Choice** run (roofing's challengeable prize). Publish policy, not commentary, to counter reputational risk: a plain "we answer to you, the homeowner — clear, documented, and honest at every step" process page, not competitor references.

## Cadence

- **Guides / cornerstone:** ~1 substantive answer-first guide per week through the build, then ~2-3/month sustained. Insurance and storm assets lead the queue.
- **Case studies:** every completed job (target at least 1/week once crews are producing photo sets), each with a review ask.
- **Storm-response:** event-triggered, not scheduled. Playbook pre-drafted and ready to publish in 24-48 hours.
- **Social:** 3 posts/week (1 completed project, 1 in-progress/crew, 1 educational), every post geo-tagged. Same assets feed GBP photos and posts.

## Storm-response playbook (pre-built, event-triggered)

Ready-to-fire template so response is measured in hours, not days:
- Blog post: "Hit by the {date} {hail/wind} storm in {towns}? Here's what to do." (the exact winning play, and the fastest way to seed the hail-damage-assessment query.) Lead the homeowner straight into the insurance path: document damage, do not sign with a storm chaser, call the advocate who meets the adjuster.
- GBP post mirroring it plus updated photos.
- Ad copy variant (only if the ads test is live).
- Review ask attached to every storm job closed.
- Trigger: any NWS-warned severe event in the county. Peak months March to November; August busiest. Lead with **wind and aging-roof** messaging: the last widespread large hail was March 2020.

## Seasonality anchors (published on-page)

"roof repair near me" peaks September, "roof leak repair" peaks January, "emergency roof repair" peaks December, broad roofing demand surges March to October. Time cost and repair content to September; winter content live by late October to index ahead of January. Water emergency content runs year-round but carries no local champion, so it can lead now.

## The one-asset-three-channels rule

Every job produces a photo set, a short video, and one homeowner takeaway, which become a website case study, a GBP post, and social posts. Every one closes with a review ask. This is the only content engine a two-crew company can actually sustain, and it doubles as the review-velocity engine: it is the plan, not a nice-to-have.
