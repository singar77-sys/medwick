# Medwick Construction: Content Calendar

Publishing plan for the rebuild, sequenced to the three Competitive Authority Reports (roofing 57.8, water 53.2, remodeling 58.8) and to the storm calendar. The build window (mid-July to mid-October 2026) sits inside peak wind season (August is busiest) and rides the September "roof repair near me" national peak. Winter leak and pipe content must index **before** the January leak-repair spike, so it ships by late October.

The single largest opening across all three markets is the same: **nobody answers the questions homeowners actually type.** The highest educational-content score in any market is a 6, and no competitor is the cited answer when a homeowner asks an AI who to call. That makes answer-engine content the #1 priority here, because one well-built asset earns both AI-search visibility and durable organic authority. Even 12 to 20 strong Medina-specific posts build a lead no incumbent can quickly close.

## Priority order

1. **AI / answer-engine educational content:** the biggest cross-market gap. Write the direct answer to the real questions homeowners type, one asset per query, per market. Water and insurance questions lead because that market is the softest and most content-dark. This is the top priority: it feeds AI-search citations and organic authority at once.
2. **Insurance-claim advocacy hub:** the verified-empty niche in both roofing and water. Everyone bills insurance; nobody owns "we fight for you." Highest strategic value after the answer-engine base.
3. **Localized cost guides:** the format 4K (65.0) proved ranks. Medina-specific, AEO-tuned pricing for water cleanup, roof replacement, basement finishing, and bathroom remodels.
4. **Emergency answer content:** water leads here (sump-pump failure, sewage backup, burst pipe first hour), then roofing emergency tarping.
5. **Storm-response posts:** within 48 hours of NWS-warned events (4K's proven accelerator).
6. **Project case studies + review asks:** every job becomes site case study, GBP post, and social, each closing with a review request. This is the review-velocity engine, not a side effect.
7. **Winter content:** roof leaks, ice dams, frozen pipes. Live by late October.
8. **Houzz cadence (remodeling, conditional):** only if the remodeling decision is yes. Mirror every remodel to Houzz and request Houzz reviews.

## How content ties to reviews and awards

Content and review velocity compound: every case study and answer post ends with a review ask, because review volume is the single fastest authority lever. The targets are concrete: beat Fixler's 34 reviews inside the first stretch, then approach SERVPRO's 189, aiming for 50 Google reviews in six months. The award pushes ride the same content: sustained local roofing content and review velocity feed the **Medina Gazette Reader's Choice** run (roofing's challengeable prize), and, if remodeling is greenlit, the Houzz cadence feeds **Best of Houzz** (the remodeling parallel). Publish policy, not commentary, to counter reputational risk: a clear "no games with your insurer" process page, not competitor references.

## Cadence

- **Guides / cornerstone:** ~1 substantive answer-first guide per week through the build, then ~2-3/month sustained.
- **Case studies:** every completed job (target at least 1/week once crews are producing photo sets), each with a review ask.
- **Storm-response:** event-triggered, not scheduled. Playbook pre-drafted and ready to publish in 24-48 hours.
- **Social:** 3 posts/week (1 completed project, 1 in-progress/crew, 1 educational), every post geo-tagged. Same assets feed GBP photos and posts.
- **Houzz (conditional):** mirror every remodel project to a Houzz portfolio entry and request a Houzz review on completion. Only runs if remodeling is greenlit.

## Storm-response playbook (pre-built, event-triggered)

Ready-to-fire template so response is measured in hours, not days:
- Blog post: "Hit by the {date} {hail/wind} storm in {towns}? Here's what to do." (4K's exact winning play, and the fastest way to seed the hail-damage-assessment query.)
- GBP post mirroring it plus updated photos.
- Ad copy variant (only if the ads test is live).
- Review ask attached to every storm job closed.
- Trigger: any NWS-warned severe event in the county. Peak months March-November; August busiest. Lead with **wind and aging-roof** messaging: the last widespread large hail was March 2020.

## Seasonality anchors (published on-page)

"roof repair near me" peaks September, "roof leak repair" peaks January, "emergency roof repair" peaks December, broad roofing demand surges March-October. Time cost and repair content to September; winter content live by late October to index ahead of January. Water emergency content runs year-round but carries no local champion, so it can lead now.

## The one-asset-three-channels rule

Every job produces a photo set, a short video, and one homeowner takeaway, which become a website case study, a GBP post, and social posts, and (if remodeling is greenlit) a Houzz entry. Every one closes with a review ask. This is the only content engine a two-crew company can actually sustain, and it doubles as the review-velocity engine: it is the plan, not a nice-to-have.
